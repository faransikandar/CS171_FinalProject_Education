Education In Another's Shoes

Understanding educational attainment globally

Educational attainment varies drastically around the world.

This is a multi-layered problem of access, quality, and incentives to attain education.3

Meet Student X.

Student X was born into a family with XYZ characteristics in the United States. She attended grade school in City A, received good grades and test scores, and participated in XYZ extracurricular activities.

She's a student at American University in Washington, D.C. She's a real person. She's just like any of us.

Now, meet Student Y.

Student Y was born into a family with XYZ characteristics in Uganda, located in East Africa. She attended grade school in City A, received good grades and test scores, and participated in XYZ extracurricular activities.

...Until age 12. At that age, her family needed her to go work on their farm and Student Y had to stop going to school.

She's currently working on her farm still - and married with two children. She's a real person. She's just like any of us.

What makes someone like Student X make it to university, while someone just like her - Student Y - in another part of the world, never gets the opportunity?1
